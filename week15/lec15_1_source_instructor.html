Team Capstone Demonstrations — Session 1
Duration: 75 minutes · Platform: Hokie AI (default thread — peer evaluation and reflection) · Submission: Thread URL submitted in Canvas before leaving class · Lecture Mode: capstone_demonstration
Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, students will be able to:

  1. Evaluate peer team capstone systems on multiple dimensions using a structured peer evaluation framework.
  2. Identify which protocol components were visibly enforced versus absent during live demonstrations.
  3. Recognize the relationship between stress testing preparation (Week 14) and actual demonstration performance.
  4. Synthesize observations across multiple demonstrations into a principled reflection on what distinguishes strong agentic systems from weaker ones.
  5. Articulate a cross-cutting design principle derived from observing multiple teams under real demonstration conditions.
📋 Note for Students

This is the first of two capstone demonstration sessions. You are attending as a peer evaluator today — your own team demonstrates in Session 2. Four or five teams will present their agentic systems via screen share. For each demonstration, complete the structured peer evaluation while the team is presenting. At the end of the session, rank the demonstrations and write a synthesis reflection on what you observed across the full session.

◉ Instructor Note

Week 15 capstone demonstrations. Session 1 covers teams Alpha through Epsilon. Each team gets 7 minutes. Students complete structured peer evaluations concurrently using their HokieAI default threads. The Q blocks are designed to capture structured observations on clarity, execution match, dimension coverage, and trust — the same dimensions covered in Weeks 11-14. After all demonstrations, students complete a brief ranking (O block) and an 11-minute synthesis reflection (R block). Circulate during demonstrations to prompt students who look disengaged or who have not opened their threads.
