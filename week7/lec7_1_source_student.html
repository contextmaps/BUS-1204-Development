Ethics Is Already There — Drift Inside the Gaps You Already Found
Duration: 75 minutes · Platform: Hokie AI · Submission: Thread URL submitted in Canvas before leaving class · Lecture Mode: exploratory
Learning Objectives

By the end of this lecture, students will be able to:

  1. Recognize ethical drift inside a task that does not announce itself as an ethics task.
  2. Identify the specific moment in an AI interaction when drift became visible.
  3. Connect a drift moment to a specific component of Protocol v1 (identity, scope, planning rule, escalation) that failed to prevent it.
  4. Explain why ambiguity in a protocol's language is itself an ethical exposure, not only a functional one.
  5. Reflect on the stance shift from "the AI made a mistake" to "my protocol did not prevent the mistake."
