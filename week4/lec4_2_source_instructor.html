Week 4 · Lecture 2
Practicing Good Interaction Habits — Clear Prompts, Fewer Retries
Duration: 75 minutes · Platform: Hokie AI · Submission: Thread URL submitted in Canvas before leaving class · Lecture Mode: consolidation
Learning Objectives

By the end of this lecture, students will be able to:

  1. Construct a clear, structured prompt using context, role, task, and constraints for a real business scenario.
  2. Evaluate prompt quality independently of output polish — identify whether the prompt or the model caused any gaps.
  3. Recognize when a prompt failure is structural rather than model-related.
  4. Identify the single most important constraint their prompt was missing and explain why it mattered.
  5. Reflect on interaction discipline as a professional habit with consequences beyond prompt quality.
📋 Note for Students

Today you write one structured prompt, run it once, and then evaluate it — not the output, the prompt itself. The rule for this session is simple: you get one prompt. No retries, no rewording, no optimization loops. Real professional workflows penalize trial-and-error. Prompt discipline starts here.

◉ Instructor Note

This is a consolidation lecture. Students explored prompt structure conceptually in Lecture 1. Today they apply it under constraint — one prompt, run once, evaluated critically. The most important shift to reinforce is that students evaluate the prompt, not the output. When circulating, ask: "Was that a prompt failure or a model failure? How do you know?" That question tends to produce the most productive analysis.

⏰ Instructor Script
  1. 0:00 Open with the rule: "Today you get one prompt. One. No retries. Design it well." Write it on the board. Explain: "The discipline of getting it right the first time is a professional skill. Every retry in a real workflow costs time and signals that you did not think carefully enough before asking."
  2. 5:00 Introduce the scenario framework. Students will choose from three business scenarios and write a structured prompt using all four elements. Walk through the scenario options briefly. Emphasize: the scenario gives them the context — their job is to add role, task, and constraints that make the prompt precise.
  3. 12:00 Students take 8 minutes to write their prompts before opening Hokie AI. They should not run anything yet. This thinking-first rule is deliberate.
  4. 20:00 Students paste T, then paste their one structured prompt. They run it once and save the output. Tell them: "Do not adjust anything. What you have is what you are working with."
  5. 25:00 M4.2.1 opens. Students evaluate their prompt against three criteria: what the prompt specified clearly, what it left vague, and whether any gap in the output was caused by the prompt or by the model. Circulate. Key question: "Find one place in the output where you can trace a gap back to something missing or ambiguous in your prompt."
  6. 40:00 Wrap M block. Students identify their single most important missing or weak constraint.
  7. 42:00 R block. 12 minutes. 80-120 words.
  8. 54:00 FAST CLASS EXTENSION: Ask students to add one sentence: "If you ran this prompt in a real professional context and shared the output without editing it, what is the most likely consequence?" This raises the professional stakes of prompt discipline.
  9. 56:00 SLOW CLASS FALLBACK: Reduce M block minimum to 3 turns. Keep the R block intact at minimum 60 words. Focus specifically on the missing constraint — that is the most important finding.
  10. 68:00 Submit thread URL to Canvas before leaving.

Opening context
Evaluate your own prompt — not the output — against three criteria. Work through each in conversation with Hokie AI. First — what the prompt specified clearly: identify which of the four structural elements (context, role, task, constraints) was strongest in your prompt and why it mattered. Second — what the prompt left vague: identify at least one place where your prompt was silent or ambiguous and ask Hokie AI what it assumed to fill that gap. Third — prompt failure versus model failure: for any gap or weakness in the output, determine whether it was caused by something missing in your prompt or by a limitation of the model. Ask Hokie AI directly: "Was there anything in my prompt that made this output less precise than it could have been?" Interact for at least 3 turns.
