[objectives]
By the end of this lecture, students will be able to:
1. Construct a clear, structured prompt using context, role, task, and constraints for a real business scenario.
2. Evaluate prompt quality independently of output polish — identify whether the prompt or the model caused any gaps.
3. Recognize when a prompt failure is structural rather than model-related.
4. Identify the single most important constraint their prompt was missing and explain why it mattered.
5. Reflect on interaction discipline as a professional habit with consequences beyond prompt quality.
[/objectives]

[instructor_script]
[0:00] Open with the rule: "Today you get one prompt. One. No retries. Design it well." Write it on the board. Explain: "The discipline of getting it right the first time is a professional skill. Every retry in a real workflow costs time and signals that you did not think carefully enough before asking."
[5:00] Introduce the scenario framework. Students will choose from three business scenarios and write a structured prompt using all four elements. Walk through the scenario options briefly. Emphasize: the scenario gives them the context — their job is to add role, task, and constraints that make the prompt precise.
[12:00] Students take 8 minutes to write their prompts before opening Hokie AI. They should not run anything yet. This thinking-first rule is deliberate.
[20:00] Students paste T, then paste their one structured prompt. They run it once and save the output. Tell them: "Do not adjust anything. What you have is what you are working with."

[42:00] R block. 12 minutes. 80-120 words.
[54:00] FAST CLASS EXTENSION: Ask students to add one sentence: "If you ran this prompt in a real professional context and shared the output without editing it, what is the most likely consequence?" This raises the professional stakes of prompt discipline.
[56:00] SLOW CLASS FALLBACK: Reduce M block minimum to 3 turns. Keep the R block intact at minimum 60 words. Focus specifically on the missing constraint — that is the most important finding.
[68:00] Submit thread URL to Canvas before leaving.

---
**AI Interaction Protocol**

In this session, you will receive blocks of content with the
following structural pattern:

```
### <BlockID>
----------
<content>
----------
###
```

Some blocks include an interaction-mode directive on a separate
line inside the dashed envelope:

```
### <BlockID>
----------
[ai_interaction=<mode>]
<content>
----------
###
```

Three modes determine your response:

  - **respond** — Engage substantively with the block content.
    Treat it as a prompt, question, or task that wants your
    analytical response. Default for S blocks (prompts), M OPEN
    blocks (multi-turn explorations), and X blocks (free
    exploration).

  - **acknowledge** — Confirm receipt with one brief sentence
    naming the block ID. Do not analyze, comment on, or expand on
    the content. The student is filing the block for the record,
    not asking for engagement. Default for T blocks (this opener),
    R blocks (reflections), M CLOSE blocks (closing markers), Q
    blocks (structured selections), and O blocks (ordering
    selections).

  - **archive** — Respond with only the block ID and nothing else.
    Used for batched archival pastes where even brief
    acknowledgment would clutter the thread.

If a block has no explicit `[ai_interaction=...]` directive, apply
the block-type default per the list above.

This protocol applies for the remainder of this session.

### M4.2.1 OPEN [mode=committed, assessment=formative | min_turns: 3]
----------
Evaluate your own prompt — not the output — against three criteria. Work through each in conversation with Hokie AI. First — what the prompt specified clearly: identify which of the four structural elements (context, role, task, constraints) was strongest in your prompt and why it mattered. Second — what the prompt left vague: identify at least one place where your prompt was silent or ambiguous and ask Hokie AI what it assumed to fill that gap. Third — prompt failure versus model failure: for any gap or weakness in the output, determine whether it was caused by something missing in your prompt or by a limitation of the model. Ask Hokie AI directly: "Was there anything in my prompt that made this output less precise than it could have been?" Interact for at least 3 turns.
----------
