---
**AI Interaction Protocol**

In this session, you will receive blocks of content with the
following structural pattern:

```
### <BlockID>
----------
<content>
----------
###
```

Some blocks include an interaction-mode directive on a separate
line inside the dashed envelope:

```
### <BlockID>
----------
[ai_interaction=<mode>]
<content>
----------
###
```

Three modes determine your response:

  - **respond** — Engage substantively with the block content.
    Treat it as a prompt, question, or task that wants your
    analytical response. Default for S blocks (prompts), M OPEN
    blocks (multi-turn explorations), and X blocks (free
    exploration).

  - **acknowledge** — Confirm receipt with one brief sentence
    naming the block ID. Do not analyze, comment on, or expand on
    the content. The student is filing the block for the record,
    not asking for engagement. Default for T blocks (this opener),
    R blocks (reflections), M CLOSE blocks (closing markers), Q
    blocks (structured selections), and O blocks (ordering
    selections).

  - **archive** — Respond with only the block ID and nothing else.
    Used for batched archival pastes where even brief
    acknowledgment would clutter the thread.

If a block has no explicit `[ai_interaction=...]` directive, apply
the block-type default per the list above.

This protocol applies for the remainder of this session.

### S12.2a.1 [type=prompt]
----------
Here is the domain task you will run through your custom agent:

TASK: Help me draft a concise business recommendation memo (3 short paragraphs) for the following scenario. The memo will summarize the situation, identify the key trade-off, and give a recommendation.

---
SCENARIO (from a startup strategy briefing):

Meridian Coffee Roasters, a regional specialty coffee company operating 12 retail locations in the mid-Atlantic, is evaluating whether to launch a direct-to-consumer subscription service. The company has strong brand recognition among local coffee enthusiasts but has no e-commerce infrastructure. The subscription model would require upfront investment in logistics software, packaging, and fulfillment operations estimated at $180,000. Their marketing director, Priya Mehta, argues the subscription model would extend brand reach beyond their regional footprint and create predictable monthly revenue. Their operations director argues that fulfillment complexity at scale could erode the premium quality perception the brand has spent eight years building. The board wants a 3-paragraph recommendation memo: paragraph one summarizes the situation and the core tension; paragraph two identifies the single most important decision criterion; paragraph three gives a clear recommendation with one condition attached.
---

Please draft the 3-paragraph memo. My agent instructions specify how you should approach this.
----------

### M12.2a.1 OPEN [mode=consolidation, assessment=formative | min_turns: 3]
----------
Phase 1 of the lab. Paste S12.2a.1 and run the initial memo task through your deployed Protocol v3 agent. Observe specifically:
- Did your agent's anticipation clause surface in the initial response — did it ask a clarifying question, flag a scope ambiguity, or make a prediction about the task before generating output?
- Did your decomposition rule produce the paragraph structure the scenario requires — is each paragraph clearly handling one sub-decision (situation summary, decision criterion, recommendation)?
- Did your trade-off articulation language guide how the agent named the tension between brand preservation and revenue growth?
After the initial response, ask a follow-up question about the agent's reasoning — why it made a particular structural choice, or how it resolved the trade-off it identified. Compare what you see to your v3 planning rule. Interact for at least 3 turns.

### M12.2a.2 OPEN [mode=consolidation, assessment=formative | min_turns: 3]
----------
Phase 2 of the lab. Push your deployed Protocol v3 agent with at least two revision requests:
- Ask for a shorter version of the memo — tighter, more executive-level.
- Ask for a restructured version that leads with the recommendation rather than the situation summary.
Observe whether your v3 sophistication holds across these revision requests: Does the decomposition hold — do the paragraphs maintain their distinct roles even when compressed or reordered? Does the anticipation clause show up in how the agent handles the structural change (does it flag anything before restructuring)? Does the trade-off articulation survive the executive compression — does the key decision criterion still appear, or does it disappear under brevity pressure? Compare Phase 2 behavior to Phase 1 behavior. Interact for at least 3 turns.
